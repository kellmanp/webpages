•The second problem we address is the variation in intensity due to the use of surface coils, which can be as much as 40% across the heart.
•Ideally, the intensities for the MI, blood, and normal tissue would be the same regardless of position, as shown in the lower figure, such that one could adjust the window-level to null either normal or blood, and operate anywhere on this curve with fairly uniform contrast.
•However, as illustrated by the top set of curves illustrating only +/-10% variation in intensity, the variation in blood for example is greater than MI-to-blood contrast.
•The top set of images are not normalized for surface coil intensity variation. You see that as the level is increased to null the blood, the weaker region of MI at the inferior wall is lost.
•In the lower set of images which are intensity normalized, the level can be adjusted to null either normal myocardium or blood while achieving a more uniform contrast.
