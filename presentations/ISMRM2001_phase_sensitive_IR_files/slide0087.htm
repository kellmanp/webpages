•This slide shows results comparing conventional magnitude images with normalized phase sensitive images.
•These images are taken at approximately the same time from dose (nominally 15 minutes) while the TI is varied.
•In the magnitude image with short TI you see the blood is nulled while the normal tissue is negative, but appears bright.
•At the longer TI, the normal tissue is approx nulled and the blood appear bright.
•For the central image the TI is somewhere in between and both normal tissue and blood appear bright.
•The phase sensitive reconstructed images have polarity restored, and after adjusting the window and level, a consistently good contrast is achieved over a wide range of TI without artifacts due to incorrect polarity.
