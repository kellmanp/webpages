This work compares the performance of 1-d and 2-d coil array geometries for SENSE accelerated volume MR imaging.
•The performance of parallel MR imaging using SENSE is highly dependent on the size, position, and number of surface coils, as well as the imaging geometry. •We evaluated performance of surface coils arrays with several geometries for performing the SENSE method for volume MR imaging with acceleration with Rate R=4. •We considered both 1-d and 2-d SENSE, and studied the effect of oblique imaging angle on the SENSE g-factor.
•I will include a few tutorial remarks and slides since this conference encompasses a wide range of imaging modalities. •Parallel imaging may be used to reduce the number of phase encodes or k-space lines acquired. •This leads to a reduction in scan time for a given spatial resolution or increased spatial resolution for a fixed scan time.
•The volume imaging parameters chosen in this study correspond to cardiac imaging •In cardiac imaging, the volume of interest is the heart which is approximately in the center of the chest and is fraction of the overall body dimension •The volume is aligned with the axis of the heart which corresponds to a doubly oblique imaging geometry
