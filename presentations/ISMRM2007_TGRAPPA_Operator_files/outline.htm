Introduction
  • TSENSE1 and TGRAPPA2 for dynamic parallel MRI have been presented previously
  • à Time-interleaved phase-encoding (PE) scheme is used for acquisition
  • à Neighboring time frames are assembled to obtain coil sensitivity information required for parallel MRI algorithm
  • à Using acceleration factor of R, at least R frames have to be assembled


  • Purpose of this work:
  • à Reduce number of time frames required for calibration process using the GRAPPA-Operator approach3
3
Review: TSENSE and TGRAPPA
  • Time-interleaved PE scheme is used for acquisition
  • Using acceleration factor of R, at least R frames have to be assembled to obtain coil sensitivity information for SENSE or GRAPPA reconstructions (see Figure)
  • Problem:  At high acceleration factors many time-frames have to be assembled
  • à Coil sensitivity information can be inconsistent for assembled frames (because of motion, for example)
4
Review: GRAPPA-Operator reconstruction
  • GRAPPA-Operator is reformulation of conventional GRAPPA
  • Missing k-space lines are reconstructed by repeated application of a single set of reconstruction parameters (coil-weights ĜPE) obtained by fitting neighboring k-space lines of calibration data set
  • Improved quality by employing multiple source points along both PE and readout dimension for reconstruction as in conventional GRAPPA4,5 (see magnified image)

Free-breathing dynamic cardiac 2D-MRI
  • Methods:
  • 1.5 T clinical MRI scanner (Siemens, Germany)
  • 32-channel cardiac array
  • TrueFISP sequence for 2D-imaging:
  • TE = 1.34 ms, TR = 2.68 ms, flip angle = 50°,
  • FOV = 360 x 360 mm2, slice thickness = 6 mm,
  • Matrix 108 x 192,
  • Time-interleaved acquisition using PE offsets 1,2,1,2,1,2,… and acceleration factor R = 4
  • à temporal resolution: 70 ms per frame (15 fps)


  • Two adjacent time-frames were used for calibration
  • GRAPPA-Operator reconstruction with 2x3 reconstruction kernel employing positive and negative shifts

Cardiac Cine 3D-MRI
  • Methods:
  • 1.5 T clinical MRI scanner (Siemens, Germany)
  • 32-channel cardiac array
  • Acceleration R = 4 x 2
9
Discussion
  • GRAPPA-Operator approach for dynamic parallel imaging
  • à Requires less adjacent time frames for obtaining reconstruction parameters
  • à More robust against motion (in particular in 3D imaging) at high accelerations
  • à Suited for minimizing transient response to changes when using acceleration factors of R>2


  • Works with conventional acquisition schemes (e.g. PE offsets 1,2,3,4,1,2,3,4,…) as well as with modified acquisition schemes (e.g. PE offsets 1,2,1,2,1,2,1,2,...)
