Echo Like Inline Display of ECG and Respiratory Signals - Enhancing Diagnostic Capabilities in Cardiac MR Imaging
2
Introduction
  • Cine MR images are used in clinical routine for cardiac function assessment.
  • In echocardiography, functional images are commonly viewed in conjunction with ECG trace to aid the interpretation.
  • MR systems are capable of recording ECG data and respiratory motion during the data acquisition.
  • Synchronized display of respiratory and ECG information with Cine MR images may provide additional clinical value.
3
Purpose
  • The goal of this study is to develop an echo-like method for simultaneous recording of ECG and respiratory data, which is integrated in the image acquisition and reconstruction of cine MR images.
  • This data is presented in synchrony as graphical overlay together with cine images.
4
Methods: Sequence Design and Implementation
  • Complete scheme is designed and integrated on a clinical scanner (MAGNETOM Avanto, Siemens, Erlangen, Germany).
  • TrueFISP based pulse sequence is modified to synchronously retrieve ECG and respiratory signal values from physiological monitoring unit (PMU) before every readout event.
  • Physiological signal information is then sent to the image calculation environment (ICE, Siemens, Erlangen, Germany) along with each line of k-space data.

6
Methods: Volunteer Study
  • Tested in healthy volunteers using free-breathing real-time and retrospectively gated segmented TrueFISP cine clinical protocols.
  • Real-time Cine: TR = 2.13 ms, TE = 0.9 ms, flip angle (FA) = 53°, slice thickness = 8 mm, matrix = 128x56, FOV = 360x258.8 mm2, GRAPPA = 2, temporal resolution (with shared phases) = 55.38 ms.
  • Segmented Cine: TR = 2.83 ms, TE = 1.2 ms, FA = 60°, slice thickness = 6 mm, matrix = 192x144, FOV = 340x255 mm2, GRAPPA = 2, temporal resolution = 38 ms (reconstructed).
  • The volunteers were asked to perform a series of different breathing maneuvers to induce heart-rate and breathing pattern variations to test reliability of this approach.
  • A wireless three lead VCG and respiratory cushion unit (Siemens) was used for cardiac and respiratory signal detection.
7
Results: Real-time Cine Acquisition
  • On the real-time cine images, an ECG trace and a respiratory signal trace are displayed for complete duration of acquisition for the corresponding slice.
  • A yellow box indicates the portion of ECG trace when data was acquired for corresponding image.
  • The red line marks the acquisition of k-space center line on both the ECG and respiratory traces.
8
Results: Segmented Cine Acquisition
  • For the segmented cine acquisitions, there is one ECG trace displayed for each heartbeat that was used in the acquisition of the slice. The respiratory trace is not displayed for segmented acquisitions.
  • The variations in R-R interval can be clearly inferred from differing lengths of traces .
9
Discussion
  • Applications:
  • Assessment of dyskinesis through synchronized ECG display.
  • Enhanced evaluation of wall motion abnormalities through true ECG indications of events within the cardiac cycle.
  • Monitoring of arrhythmia and its impact on image quality.
  • Monitoring heart rate changes with different respiratory states – e.g. autonomic reflex assessment in the Valsalva maneuver.
  • Retrospective k-space editing based on irregular physiological trace patterns such as arrhythmia (already in use with CT to improve image quality).
10
Conclusions
  • Recording of ECG and respiratory data within cine images was successfully demonstrated in healthy volunteers.
  • Visual assessment of the overlaid images confirms the correspondence between image appearance and ECG/Respiratory states.
  • This physiological information can aid in assessment of dyskinesis, in monitoring of arrhythmia or in ECG monitoring during pharmacological wall motion stress MRI.
  • Accuracy of signal detection must be evaluated in further studies.
