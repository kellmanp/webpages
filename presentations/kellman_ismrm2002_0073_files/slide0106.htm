Motivation
•ECG gated, segmented cardiac imaging using FISP commonly uses a continuous train of RF pulses, and imaging is performed essentially at steady state after 1 or more RF pulses during steady state approach are discarded.
•Applications requiring precise triggering or additional magnetization preparations such as tagging or fat saturation interrupt the steady state and create ghost artifacts during the first few cardiac phases during early systole.
•I will describe a method for eliminating these ghosts which occur during the approach to interrupted steady state.
