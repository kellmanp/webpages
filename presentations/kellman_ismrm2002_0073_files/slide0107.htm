Sequence Timing
•The sequence timing is shown in this slide
•The ECG R-wave is used as a trigger followed by an optional magnetization preparation for either tagging or fat saturation.
•A single alpha/2 pulse is used to accelerate the approach to steady state, followed by the segmented acquisition.
•A single alpha/2 pulse is used to store the magnetization prior to a small trigger window, followed by a crusher to destroy any residual transverse magnetization.
•After the 1st heartbeat which is discarded, the interrupted steady state transient response is essentially the same from beat to beat.
•In this example there are 4 phase encode lines acquired per cardiac phase corresponding to approximately 16 ms temporal resolution.
