How Phased Array
Ghost Elimination (PAGE) Works?
•Let me begin by describing how the basic ghost elimination method (referred to as PAGE) works.
•In phased array or multi-coil reception, the desired image as well as ghosts are weighted by the coil sensitivity for individual coils as depicted for this example with 4 coils and FOV/2 ghosts.
•The individual coils sensitivity weights both the desired image as well as ghosts, denoted here by si(x,y).
•This weighted superposition is a set of linear equations which can be solved (in a least square sense) by a matrix inversion if the sensitivity profiles are known.
•The desired image and ghost are also weighted by their respective values of complex point spread function, h0 and h1 in this case. Therefore, the equations actually solve for  the PSF weighted desired and ghost images, h0 f  and h1 f.
•Unlike the SENSE accelerated imaging application for which the ghosts are caused by intentional undersampling with a space invariant PSF, this method is quite general and may be used with a spatially varying PSF. That is to say h0 and h1 are functions of (x,y).
