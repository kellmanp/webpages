•I will present an application of phase array ghost elimination to cancel hosts that arise in cardiac imaging using true-triggered FISP during the approach to interrupted steady state.
•ECG gated, segmented cardiac imaging using FISP commonly uses a continuous train of RF pulses, and imaging is performed essentially at steady state after 1 or more RF pulses during steady state approach are discarded. •Applications requiring precise triggering or additional magnetization preparations such as tagging or fat saturation interrupt the steady state and create ghost artifacts during the first few cardiac phases during early systole. •I will describe a method for eliminating these ghosts which occur during the approach to interrupted steady state.
•Let me begin by describing how the basic ghost elimination method (referred to as PAGE) works. •In phased array or multi-coil reception, the desired image as well as ghosts are weighted by the coil sensitivity for individual coils as depicted for this example with 4 coils and FOV/2 ghosts. •The individual coils sensitivity weights both the desired image as well as ghosts, denoted here by si(x,y). •This weighted superposition is a set of linear equations which can be solved (in a least square sense) by a matrix inversion if the sensitivity profiles are known. •The desired image and ghost are also weighted by their respective values of complex point spread function, h0 and h1 in this case. Therefore, the equations actually solve for  the PSF weighted desired and ghost images, h0 f  and h1 f. •Unlike the SENSE accelerated imaging application for which the ghosts are caused by intentional undersampling with a space invariant PSF, this method is quite general and may be used with a spatially varying PSF. That is to say h0 and h1 are functions of (x,y).

•The ECG R-wave is used as a trigger followed by an optional magnetization preparation for either tagging or fat saturation. •A single alpha/2 pulse is used to accelerate the approach to steady state, followed by the segmented acquisition. •A single alpha/2 pulse is used to store the magnetization prior to a small trigger window, followed by a crusher to destroy any residual transverse magnetization. •After the 1st heartbeat which is discarded, the interrupted steady state transient response is essentially the same from beat to beat. •In this example there are 4 phase encode lines acquired per cardiac phase corresponding to approximately 16 ms temporal resolution.
•The k-space distortion is periodic leading to image ghosts during the approach to steady state. •In this example, with 4 phase encodes lines acquired per cardiac phase, the k-space distortion has a period of 4, which results in 4 ghosts spaced FOV/4. •The simulated transient approach to interrupted steady state with alpha/2 preparation is shown for fat tissue at off-resonance.

•A block diagram of the phase array ghost elimination method illustrates the processing steps. •A sequence of complex multi-coil images are reconstructed including the images with ghosts during the initial cardiac phases. •Ghost free images after steady state is reached are averaged and used for the estimating the B1-maps which are used in calculating the phased array combiner coefficients. •Phased array combining is applied to the full-FOV images at each shift to separate the superimposed (ghost) images. •The individual images are each weighted by their (space variant) point spread function value, h0(x,y), h1, h2, & h3, respectively. •These separated images may be combined to produce a final ghost artifact cancelled image.
