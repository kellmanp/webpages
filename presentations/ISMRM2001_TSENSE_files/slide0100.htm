Temporal Spectra
myocardium region
•The artifact suppression may be analyzed quantitatively by examining the temporal spectrum.
•This spectrum corresponds to the myocardium region with most severe chest wall ghost. The desired myocardium component in the center, which is modulated at the heart rate, is approximately 10 dB weaker than the intense chest wall component which is temporally shifted to the bandedge, in this case +/- 15.6 Hz.
•Note that the chest wall component is actually modulated at the heart rate as well, due to the heart thumping on the chest and possible blood flow. This modulation, while weak, is not readily filtered by temporal filtering, and is the source of artifacts when using temporal filtering such as UNFOLD by itself.
