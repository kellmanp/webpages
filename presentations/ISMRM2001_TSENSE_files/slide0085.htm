Higher Accelerations
(R=4 examples)
Let me speak briefly about extension to higher rates of acceleration
The are numerous combinations of phase encode order and spatio-temporal filtering. Let me give a few examples which achieve an overall R-4 acceleration.
For R=4, every 4-th phase encode is acquired.
If we alternate every 2 frames with an offset of 2-lines, the odd ghosts at 1/4 FOV and 3/4 FOV are temporally shifted to the bandedge, while the FOV/2 does not experience any phase shift and remains at d.c.. In this case SENSE may be used to cancel the 1/2 FOV ghost and temporal filtering such as UNFOLD may be used to cancel the odd ghosts. I presented experimental results for this combination last year. This scheme is not adaptive since it cannot be used to derive a ghost free reference and has a very constrained dynamic FOV.
An alternate approach is to cycle every 4-frames stepping a single line each frame, thus acquiring all k-space lines every 4-frames. This scheme may clearly be used for adpative TSENSE. Each ghosts is temporally shifted to a different frequency as depicted. The 1/2 FOV ghost will be at the bandedge, while the odd ghosts at 1/4 FOV and 3/4 FOV will be at quarterband.
While one could perform R=4 SENSE and R=4 UNFOLD, using a comb notch, the use of notch filters is not particularly desirable, and furthermore, the outer 3/4 FOV would still be constrained to be relatively static.
Another approach is to temporally filter only the bandedge removing the 1/2 FOV ghosts. Either a R=3 or 4 SENSE may then be used. With R=3, the outer 1/2 FOV must be relatively static, but the loss in SNR due to variance inflation (so called G-factor) will be improved.
