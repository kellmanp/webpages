•This simplified diagram illustrates the TSENSE method for a rate 2 acceleration.
•The k-space is acquired with multiple coils using an interleaved phase encode order, odd-even-odd-even, etc.
•The reduced FOV images may then be reconstructed with SENSE
•Any residual artifacts due to errors in coil sensitivity estimates will be temporal frequency shifted to the band-edge, and can be further suppressed using a temporal lowpass filter. The order of temporal filter and SENSE may of course be reversed.
•The coil sensitivity maps may be estimated adaptively as shown using the surface coil images alone (without acquiring separate body coil reference acquisitions), by using a temporal lowpass filter.
•The narrower lowpass filter bandwidth can be used to produce highly ghost suppressed images with lower temporal resolution, which may be used to compute the SENSE coefficients. I’ll say more about this in a few moments.
•Example thumbnail images are shown to illustrate the method. Better quality images will be shown in a moment, along with quantitative measurements of artifact suppression.
•This example is for  non-breath-held, continuous,  real time imaging of a short axis slice of the heart, immediately following exercise stress, so the heart is beating at 150 bpm and the respiratory rate is 30 breaths/min, with heavy chest breathing. This was conducted on a GE scanner using their standard cardiac 4-surface coil array.
•You can observe the beating between the image and ghost in the magnitude images, caused by the alternating phase of the alias ghost.
•After lowpass filtering the ghosts are suppressed.
•The adaptive SENSE images achieve the full temporal resolution, and have reasonable quality, but there is still some residual alias artifact since the chest wall is moving so rapidly, and there are errors in measuring the relative coil sensitivities. You can see that the residual artifact is flickering, since it is shifted to the bandedge.
•This residual artifact is further suppressed using a temporal lowpass filter with 80% bandwidth.
