Short Axis Image Example
exercise induced stress at HR=150 bpm
•These short axis images were acquired approximately 15 seconds after exercise stress.
•They are acquired continuously at 31 frames per second in real-time during heavy breathing using a FGRE sequence with an ETL=8. The spatial resolution was 128 x 96  with 1/2 phase FOV acquiring 48 ky-lines, 24 even and 24 odd, and so on.
•The alias images are shown for reference to illustrate how intense the surface coil intensity is at the chest wall, which requires a high degree of suppression.
•The adaptive SENSE and UNFOLD methods applies individually achieve a high degree of artifact suppression, nevertheless there is residual artifact at particular phases.
•The adaptive TSENSE with combined spatio-temporal filtering is able to achieve additional artifact suppression.
