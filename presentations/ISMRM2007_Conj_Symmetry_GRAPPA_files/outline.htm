Introduction
  • Combination of parallel MRI with Partial-Fourier approaches
  • à Has been presented previously1,2,3
  • à Utilize conjugate k-space symmetry
  • à Require phase-maps


  • Purpose of this work:
  • Utilize conjugate k-space symmetry for improved reconstruction quality in parallel MRI experiments featuring GRAPPA
  • à Generate virtual coils containing conjugate symmetric signal from actual coils
  • à Works with symmetric trajectories (in contrast to Partial Fourier applications)
  • à No phase-map required

Theory

  • with spin-density distribution r(x) and FT[.] being the Fourier-transform



  • Conjugate symmetric signal:


  • à Corresponds to signal received with complex conjugate coil sensitivity (assuming non-complex spin-density distribution)
4
Theory
  • Basic Idea:
  • Generate virtual coils containing conjugate symmetric signal from actual coils:



  • à Virtual coils provide extra encoding power because of additional information
  • from complex conjugate coil sensitivities
  • à Improved reconstruction quality (i.e. reduced geometry factor)


  • Requirements:
    • Symmetric k-space acquisition
    • Calibration data for GRAPPA reconstruction process obtained with same sequence parameters
5
Computer Simulation
  • Methods:
  • Sensitivity profiles of a four-channel linear array were computed
  • Numerical phantom with linear phase roll over FOV was multiplied
  • Gaussian white noise was added to k-space signal
  • Virtual coils were generated as described in Theory section
  • GRAPPA reconstructions (acceleration factor    R = 3) were performed using 11 ACS lines
  • ACS lines were not integrated into final reconstruction


  • Results:
  • Reconstructions using conjugate symmetry exhibit less noise enhancement (see Figure 1)
6
Spin-Echo Experiment (Acceleration R = 2.8)
  • Methods:
  • 1.5 T clinical scanner (Siemens, Germany)
  • 12-channel head coil
  • Spin-echo sequence TE / TR = 7.8ms / 500 ms
  • FOV 200 mm x 230 mm, slice = 5 mm
  • Matrix 224 x 256
  • GRAPPA reconstructions (outer reduction factor    R = 4) were performed using 31 ACS lines
  • ACS lines were not integrated into final reconstruction


  • Results:
  • Reconstructions using conjugate symmetry (Figure 2, bottom row) exhibit less noise enhancement than the conventional GRAPPA reconstruction (Figure 2, top row).
7
Free-breathing Cardiac MRI with TGRAPPA
  • Methods:
  • Clinical 1.5 T MRI scanner (Siemens, Germany)
  • 8-channel cardiac array
  • TrueFISP (balanced SSFP) sequence:
  • TE = 1.1 ms, TR = 2.2 ms,  flip  angle = 50°, FOV = 285 x 380 mm2, slice thickness = 6 mm, Matrix = 96 x 128, phase resolution = 63%
  • Time-interleaved acquisition (acceleration R=4)
  • Temporal resolution: 34 ms
  • (corresponding to 29 frames per second)


  • Results:
  • Reconstructions using conjugate symmetry  (Figure 3, bottom row) exhibit less noise enhancement and reveal more details than conventional TGRAPPA (Figure 3, top row)
8
Discussion
  • Practical approach for utilizing conjugate k-space symmetry
  • à Signal from virtual coils provide extra sensitivity encoding power
  • à Improved GRAPPA reconstructions (less noise enhancement)
  • à No phase-maps are required


  • Works with symmetric k-space trajectories
  • à Could be extended for symmetric non-Cartesian trajectories (e.g. time-interleaved radial MRI)


  • Auto-Calibration data have to be obtained with same sequence parameters
  • à Variable density acquisition or time-interleaved phase-encoding advantageous
  • Limitation: increased computation time because of increased number of coils
9
Conclusions
  • Conjugate k-space symmetry can be used for improved GRAPPA reconstructions of data acquired with symmetric trajectories
  • The presented approach does not require phase-maps
