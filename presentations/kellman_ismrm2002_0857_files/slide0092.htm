- Overall array performance
-all arrays tested had same SNR in central region (within 15%)
-reasonable agreement with simulation
-good pre-amp decoupling achieved
-Cardiac SENSE performance
-  8 coils provided reasonable quality up to R=3 acceleration
     increased robustness to differences in coil positioning or slice orientation
-  4 coils degraded rapidly for R>2 due to  ill-conditioning
     high sensitivity to B1-map errors reduced artifact suppression
Conclusions
•The SNR of the myocardium in the posterior wall of the LV which represents the worst case region was approximately the same for arrays tested, which was in reasonable agreement with simulations.
•A high level of isolation between coils was achieved and did not limit degrade performance.
•Using the 8-element surface coil arrays, cardiac imaging using SENSE was robust and had reasonable quality up to rate 3 with the imaging parameters used.
• Using 4 coils, the SENSE performance degraded for rates higher than 2, as expected.
