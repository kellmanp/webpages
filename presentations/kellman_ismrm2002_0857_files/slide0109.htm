SENSE g-factor (R=2)
doubly oblique geometry for SAX slice
•The g-factor represents the loss in SNR above the √R loss due to acceleration which results from ill-conditioning of the inverse solution.
•The g-factor depends depend on the positions and sizes of coil elements as well geometry of the imaging plane.
•The value of the g-factor varies across the FOV and may have significant hot spots.
•A simulation was performed to calculate the g-factor while varying a number of parameters.
•The examples shown here (for R=2 SENSE) illustrate the g-factor for the 3 different array configurations previously described for a doubly oblique imaging plane typical of cardiac imaging of a short axis slice.
•The g-factor for the 8-element linear array shown in the left is below 1.05 over the central region of interest, while the 4-element array has hot spots on the order of 1.25 for the same imaging plane.
