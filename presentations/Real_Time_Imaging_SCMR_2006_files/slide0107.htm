Conclusion
Real time benefits
       rapid, simple acquisition
       clinically useful for arrhythmia cases

Limitations
compromised spatial & temporal resolution
 requires optimized coils
In  conclusion, despite its limitations of compromised spatial and temporal resolution, and the need for optimized coils, real-time imaging has benefits over conventional  segmented cine-imaging.  It has proven clinically useful in  patients with arrhythmias and is also useful for cases such as pediatric or heart failure, or any other condition where breath holding is a problem. It is a simple, rapid method of acquisition, capable of  obtaining an entire stack of images in tens of seconds.
