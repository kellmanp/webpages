Segmented SSFP is the traditional method of acquisition for cine imaging in cardiac MRI and works very well for patients with regular heartbeat rythm. Segmented scans are breath held, ECG gated, over multiple heart beats.   With each heartbeat we obtain a set number of phase encode lines, based on the  views-per-segment.  To keep it simple, this example show  4 PE lines acquired each heartbeat.   During the first heartbeat, we obtain the first  4 lines of K-space at each phase of the cardiac cycle.  During the second heartbeat we obtain the  4 next lines, until we have filled the matrix. The resultant cine images provide high quality with good spatial and temporal resolution.

Patients with arrhythmias present a challenge in obtaining segmented images.    In most cases images are degraded by artifacts caused by inconsistent cardiac shape, size and motion during abnormal heart beats.   The lower diagram demonstrates segmented imaging in a patient with arrhythmia,  which causes image blurring artifacts.
