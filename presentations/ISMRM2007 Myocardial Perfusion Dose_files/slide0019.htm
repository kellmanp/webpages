Discussion
nSince apparent myocardial gadolinium concentration did not double as expected with the increase in contrast dose, clipping of the myocardial signal intensity is likely a problem with the full-dose contrast using the selected imaging parameters.
n
nMyocardial signal intensity clipping is not due to the nonlinear relationship between T1 and gadolinium concentration. We speculate that interstitial loading or T2* effects within intramyocardial blood vessels are important factors affecting myocardial signal intensity.
