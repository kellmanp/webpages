Introduction
nSpecific Aims
nTo determine if apparent myocardial contrast concentrations during first-pass perfusion MR imaging is proportional to gadolinium dose.
nTo determine how apparent myocardial contrast concentrations vary between rest and dipyridamole stress perfusion.
n
nBackground
nQuantification of first-pass contrast-enhanced perfusion MR imaging depends on measuring signal intensity of the LV and myocardium.
nThis T1-related signal intensity is affected by factors including gadolinium concentration, T2*, pulse sequence, sequence parameters, contrast dose, perfusion, water exchange rates, and interstitial loading with gadolinium.
nApparent myocardial contrast concentration can be simulated from MR signal intensity based on magnetization modeling.
