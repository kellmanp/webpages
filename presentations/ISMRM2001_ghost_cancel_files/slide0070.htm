SNR Loss
using ghost cancellation method
•The ghost cancellation method does incur a penalty in SNR.
•The SNR loss or variance inflation is due to the ill-conditioning of the matrix inverse required to separate the ghosts, just as in the SENSE accelerated imaging method, except without an additional acceleration loss. The SNR loss depends on a number of factors including the number and spacing of ghosts, the number of coils and specific coil sensitivity profiles, the slice geometry, and noise statistics.
•The SNR loss is spatially varying.
•For the 4 coil system that we used, the SNR loss for ETL=2 (FOV/2 ghosts) was in the range of 10 to 40% depending on the image location.
•For ETL=3 the SNR loss with 4 coils was several fold higher.
•In the future, using 6 to 8 coils, we should be able to use ETL=3 or 4 with acceptable losses.
