Simplified Block Diagram
(FOV/2 ghost example)
•This block diagram illustrates the phased array ghost cancellation method for a FOV/2 ghost example.
•This example uses an oil/water phantom which is acquired using multi-shot EPI with a short echotrain length of 2, and a non-interleaved phase encode order to illustrate the cancellation of space variant ghosts. I will discuss more about phase encode strategies in a few moments.
•The strong oil ghost is due to the chemical shift, while the much weaker water ghost is due to the slight off-resonance due to shimming errors.
•On the top are the multi-coil images with ghosts and on the bottom are the same images shifted by the FOV/2 ghost separation.
•After phased array processing the superimposed image and ghosts are separated to produce images weighted by their respective point spread values. At the top the ghost is nulled, while the bottom nulls the desired image.
•The 2 PSF weighted images may be combined either coherently or non-coherently to form a final image. In the case of coherent combining the complex weights must be estimated.
