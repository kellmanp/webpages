•Before showing results, let me say a few words about comparing the effect of phase encode order on distortion.
•This example is for short echo train multi-shot EPI using an echo train length of 4, which is typical in rapid cardiac imaging where echo train length are kept small due to T2* limitations.
•These graphs illustrate the off-resonance phase versus ky for various phase encode orders, and their respective point spread functions.
•For interleaved acquisition without echo-shifting, off-resonance gives rise to significant distortion.
•This is mitigated with echo-shifting by smoothing the distortion at the price of extending the TR and there is still some geometric distortion remaining.
•A non-interleaved or sequential order has rapid periodic phase errors which results in widely spaced ghosts.
•Other distortion mechanisms listed which lead to blurring or smearing with the interleaved order, give rise to widely spaced ghosts with the non-interleaved order.
