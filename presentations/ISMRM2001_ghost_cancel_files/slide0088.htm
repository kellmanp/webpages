Comparison of strategies
(multi-shot EPI example)
•This leads to a new strategy which I illustrated earlier when describing the block diagram used for phased array ghost cancellation.
•By selecting a phase encode order such as non-interleaved, the k-space distortion due to a wide variety of mechanisms will be rapid and periodic leading to widely spaced ghosts. These ghosts can in turn be cancelled with the phased array method.
•In addition to a reduction in TR, thus improved scan efficiency, there is also a reduction in distortion.
•You can see by comparison that using the conventional interleaved echo shifted method, the oil region has a small chemical shift in the phase encode direction, that is eliminated by using the strategy of non-interleaved acquisition and ghost cancellation.
•EPI flow artifacts using interleaved order are also translated to widely spaced ghosts which may then be cancelled.
