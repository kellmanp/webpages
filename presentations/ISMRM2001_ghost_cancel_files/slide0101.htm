Multi-Echo FISP Example
(ETL=2)
•We applied the adaptive phased array ghost cancellation method to cardiac imaging using a gated segmented cine acquisition.
•The pulse sequence used FISP to gain increased blood to myocardium contrast, and we used echo trains of both 2 and 3 echoes to increase the scan efficiency which may be traded off for a greater number of cardiac phases, reduced breath-hold time, or increased spatial resolution.
•In the upper left the images with ghosts are shown, and along side are the separated images weighted by h0 and h1, the space variant point spread function. The ghosts are clearly greater in regions of fat.
•The h0 and h1 weighted images may be combined as shown, or a small degree of fat suppression may be achieved by simply using the h0 weighted image by itself.
•The interleaved echo time shifted image is shown for comparison. This method has increased TR with slightly reduced scan efficiency, greater FISP off-resonance banding artifacts, and greater flow artifacts, due largely to the larger phase encode blip size.
