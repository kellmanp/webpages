The matrix formulation and solution (following Pruessmann, et al.*) incorporates both Fourier (gradient) and coil sensitivity encoding for arbitary k-space acquisition. In this formulation the matrix-vector notation assumes that 2d variables are ordered in a 1-d vector. In other words, the column vector r consists of nr=N2 elements over (x,y), and likewise, k, assumes nk values over (kx,ky). The column vector G consists of measured k-space data for nc coils, i.e., nc nk x1. The equation G = E f + n represents the encoding or forward problem.

Throughout the presentation, it is assumed that the coils sensitivities, si are known or can be estimated with sufficient accuracy. The subject of in-vivo estimation of coil sensitivities  or auto-calibration is very important and will be addressed in the next session.

*Pruessmann KP, Weiger M, Scheidegger MB, Boesiger P. SENSE: Sensitivity encoding for fast MRI. Magn Reson Med 1999:42(5):952-962.
