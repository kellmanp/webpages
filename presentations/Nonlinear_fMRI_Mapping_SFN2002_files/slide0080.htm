Previous studies* have shown:
non-linearities in electrical activity
ERG: Sutter (1987)
EEG: Baseler & Sutter (1997)
Intra-cellular: Benardete & Victor (1994)
non-linearities in BOLD fMRI
Vasques & Noll (1998)
Boynton, et al. (1996)
Birn, et al. (2001)
Friston, et al. (1998)
* key publication dates
•Previous studies have reported non-linearity in measured electrical activity as well as non-linearity in BOLD fMRI.
•Non-linear response to visual stimulus activation have been measured using electro-retinograms, surface electrodes using EEG, and intracellular recordings.
•Non-linearities in BOLD fMRI were reported by Vasques & Noll as well as Boynton who observed deviation from linear superposition when varying the stimulus duration. Birn, et al. reported a spatial heterogeneity of the non-linear response using a similar measurement method. Friston, et al. characterized the BOLD fMRI non-linearity by estimating Volterra kernel coefficients using a basis fitting approach, and concluded that the non-linearity was predominately caused by the hemodyamic effect.
