•Raw correlation waveforms are shown for a single pixel in the fovea on the left and a single pixel in the periphery on the right.
•The top traces show the raw correlation for both m-sequence and inverse repeat overlaid.
•The 1st order response or difference is shown in the middle row. The 1st order response is an estimate of the hemodynamic response function which is delayed 3 to 4 seconds and has a several second duration. The 1st order response also has a characteristic undershoot.
•The 2nd order response or sum is shown in the bottom trace. The 2nd order response is negative and correlates at a delay offset which is determined by the specific m-sequence used.
•Note that the higher relative strength of the 2nd order response in the periphery and higher relative strength of the 1st order response in the fovea.
