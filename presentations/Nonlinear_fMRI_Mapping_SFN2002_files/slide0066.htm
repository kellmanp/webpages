•Various fMRI visual paradigms were used in conjunction with the binary m-sequence probe.
•In one experiment a bulleye and its reverse were presented in correspondence to a 1 and 0 of the m-sequence.
•In a 2nd experiment, a 0 corresponded to a uniform gray with mean luminance equal to that of the bullseye after gamma correction for the display.
•In a 3rd experiment, black & white uniform disks were presented.
•A conventional block design paradigm alternating between annular and central disk regions of bullseye reversals was used to define foveal and peripheral regions for measuring average correlation scores.
