•The results of correlation analysis are shown here for a normal subject using the bullseye-gray paradigm.
•The upper left image displays the image intensity to serve as an anatomical reference.
•The lower left is a map of the t-score from the block design where the dark region corresponds to the fovea and the light region corresponds to the periphery (2 to 8 degrees eccentricity)
•(B) and (E) are the 1st and 2nd order response maps without a gap, and (C) and (F) are the 1st and 2nd order responses with a 200 ms gap. As predicted, the 2nd order response is greatly attenuated with the gap indicating the 2nd order nonlinear response is predominantly due to the fast neuronal response.
•Note that there is also a trend that the fovea has a stronger 1st order response than the periphery, while the periphery has a stronger 2nd order response than the fovea.
