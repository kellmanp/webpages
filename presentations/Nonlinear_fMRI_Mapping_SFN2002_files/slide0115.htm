Using m-sequence probes, we estimate the Wiener kernels by cross correlation.
Using the cycle-and-add property of m-sequences you can see that the product of a ±1 m-sequence with a delayed version produces a shifted version of the same sequence.
Thus the 2nd order kernel produces a response in the shifted m-sequence correlation. By judicious choice of m-sequence, it is possible to measure the 1st and 2nd order kernels without overlap.
The full period m-sequence correlation illustrates the response to 1st and 2nd order kernels using a 255-length sequence.
The inverse repeat method may be used to further separate even and odd order responses.
