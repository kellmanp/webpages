BOLD fMRI with minimal gradient acoustic noise


Jacco A. de Zwart, Peter van Gelderen, Peter Kellman, Jeff H. Duyn

Advanced MRI, LFMI, NINDS, and
LCE, NHLBI, National Institutes of Health, Bethesda, MD, USA

Objective
Reducing gradient acoustic noise in EPI through slew-rate reduction using sensitivity-encoding (SENSE).

Introduction
In EPI, oscillatory gradient switching patterns generate high sound pressure levels (SPLs) in the human auditory frequency range. This not only causes patient discomfort and distress, but can also interfere with the experiment, particularly fMRI studies of the auditory cortex.
SPLs can be reduced through gradient coil design, sound absorbing materials, active sound cancelation, and gradient wave-form design. Here, SENSE [1] was used to reduce SPL through slew-rate reduction. For a given image matrix size, SENSE allows fewer k-space lines to be acquired. Therefore, for a given data acquisition window duration, data can be acquired with lower bandwidth, greatly reducing slew-rate (R2 slew-rate reduction for R-fold under-sampling). SNR is expected to suffer only a spatially dependent, SENSE-reconstruction related penalty (g-factor [1]), which can be small (<10%) depending on experimental conditions (e.g. coil configuration, acceleration rate).
Feasibility of SENSE for fMRI has been demonstrated for reduced acquisition time [2,3] and spatial resolution-increase [3]. Here, sensitivity in fMRI experiments using reduced-SPL SENSE-EPI was compared to conventional-EPI. Achieved SPL-reduction was measured for three scanner platforms.

Materials & Methods
SPLs were measured for conventional-EPI, rate-2 and rate-3 SENSE-EPI on GE Signa LX (1.5 and 3.0 T) and 1.5 T Siemens Magnetom using a Sper Scientific 840029 SPL meter (dB(A) & "slow" response settings), placed at scanner-bed height, 3.5 m from the magnet iso-center. Scan parameters (conventional-EPI): 220165 mm2 field-of-view; 2000 ms TR; 40 ms TE; twelve 4-mm thick slices; 1 mm slice gap; 6448 matrix; 50% ramp sampling; 26.7 mT.m-1 read-out gradient amplitude; 124 s gradient flat time; 180 s ramp time; 250 kHz bandwidth. Parameters different for SENSE (rate-2/rate-3): 22083/22055 mm2 field-of-view; 13.4/8.9 mT.m-1 read-out amplitude; 248/372 s flat; 360/540 s ramps; 125/83 kHz bandwidth.
Finger tapping experiments were performed on normal volunteers (n=6) on the 1.5 T GE using conventional and rate-2 SENSE EPI. Two pairs of data were acquired with scan parameters as above. 180 volumes were acquired (1-minute stabilization followed by 5 30/30 s activation/rest). A Nova Medical 4-element dome coil was used. Data were acquired and processed as described in [3].

Results & Discussion
Table 1 shows SPLs measured at three MRI-platforms. Rate-2 SENSE (4-fold-reduced slew-rate) led on average to a 11.3 dB(A) SPL-reduction, rate-3 SENSE (9-fold-reduced slew-rate) to 16.5 dB(A) average reduction. SPL from gradients other than read-out averaged 72.9 dB(A).
Averaged over all volunteers, fMRI results did not differ significantly between conventional- and SENSE-EPI. The average t-score in the primary motor cortex (PMC) was 5.592.53 for conventional- and 5.811.85 for SENSE-EPI. Temporal signal standard-deviation in the PMC-voxels was 1.37%0.22 (conventional) and 1.29%0.36 (SENSE). In PMC, the average g-factor was 1.04 (4% SENSE-related SNR-loss), average SNR was 190.5.

Conclusion
SENSE-MRI can be sucessfully applied for significant reduction of gradient acoustic noise in EPI with only minor SNR penalty.
